  • [PAUSE] --- ## 2) What Is Prosthodontics? Scope and Purpose Prosthodontics is the dental specialty focused on the diagnosis, planning, rehabilitation, and maintenance of oral function, comfort, appearance, and health—using dental prostheses.

Key Points 5

  • At its core, prosthodontics answers three big questions: - What is missing or failing? - What does the patient need to function comfortably and confidently? - What design best respects biology while managing forces? Prosthodontic treatment may be fixed—like crowns and bridges—or removable—like partial and complete dentures—and it often overlaps with implant dentistry, occlusion, esthetics, and temporomandibular disorder management.
  • [PAUSE] ### Quick Clinical Anchor Imagine a patient who says: “Doctor, I don’t want dentures.” That statement might sound like a final decision—but in prosthodontics, our job is to translate that preference into realistic options: fixed bridges, implants, or a removable prosthesis designed for comfort and appearance.
  • The key is aligning patient goals with clinical feasibility.
