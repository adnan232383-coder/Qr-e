Key Points 4

  • Think of every implant case as a triangle: - Biology: bone quality/quantity, healing capacity, systemic risk - Mechanics: occlusion, load, implant design, restorative material - Esthetics/Soft tissue: tissue phenotype, papillae, emergence profile, smile line If one corner is neglected, complications show up somewhere else.
  • Risk Assessment: The “Before You Touch a Drill” Questions Start with high-yield risk screens: - Smoking/vaping: delayed healing, increased peri-implantitis risk - Diabetes control: HbA1c, wound healing reliability - History of periodontitis: higher peri-implant disease risk; demands strict maintenance - Parafunction: bruxism increases mechanical overload complications - Medications: antiresorptives, immunosuppressants, anticoagulants—plan accordingly [EMPHASIS] Key concept: Implant placement is prosthetically driven.
  • The implant is not the goal—the restoration is.
