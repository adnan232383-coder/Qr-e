  • [PAUSE] --- ## 2) Section One — Advanced Diagnosis & Decision-Making Frameworks Let’s start with an area where exam questions love to live: diagnosis and treatment planning.
  • A consistent approach is to separate problems into three domains: - **Pulpal diagnosis**: normal, reversible pulpitis, symptomatic irreversible pulpitis, asymptomatic irreversible pulpitis, necrosis - **Apical diagnosis**: normal apical tissues, symptomatic apical periodontitis, asymptomatic apical periodontitis, acute apical abscess, chronic apical abscess - **Restorability and periodontal status**: because a perfectly done root canal on a non-restorable tooth is still a poor outcome.
  • [PAUSE] ### Key advanced diagnostic pitfalls - **Referred pain**: mandibular molars can refer to ear, temple, or opposing arch.
