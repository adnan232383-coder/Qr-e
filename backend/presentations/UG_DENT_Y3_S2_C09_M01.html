  • In this module, we’ll build the foundation you’ll use every time you select, manipulate, and evaluate dental materials in clinic.
  • In Dental Materials I, you likely focused on core properties and basic categories.

Key Points 2

  • Dental Materials II takes that knowledge and connects it more directly to **clinical decision-making**—why a material works, when it fails, and how technique and environment influence outcomes.
  • Dental materials aren’t just “products.” They’re engineered systems that interact with teeth, saliva, occlusal forces, bacteria, temperature changes, and—most importantly—your handling.
  • A great material used poorly can fail.

Key Points 3
