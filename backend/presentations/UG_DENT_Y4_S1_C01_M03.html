  • [PAUSE] --- ## 2) Section One — “Think Like a Periodontist”: Advanced Diagnosis & Prognosis Let’s start with the foundation: diagnosis and prognosis.
  • In advanced periodontology, the hardest part is rarely “spot the pocket.” It’s deciding what the pockets *mean* in the context of the whole patient.
  • A high-yield way to think is: - **Disease severity and complexity**: probing depths, CAL, radiographic bone loss pattern, furcation involvement, mobility, occlusal trauma signs - **Risk and modifiers**: smoking, diabetes, stress, genetics, medications, plaque control - **Patient-level prognosis** and **tooth-level prognosis**: treatability, compliance potential, strategic value of teeth [PAUSE] ### Key Advanced Concept: Staging and Grading Integration Staging tells you “how much damage” and “how complex.” Grading tells you “how fast” and “how risky” moving forward.
