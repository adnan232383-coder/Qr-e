3) Key Terms: Speaking the Language of Endodontics

Before we go further, let’s clarify key terms you’ll hear throughout this course.

Core Anatomy & Tissues

[EMPHASIS] **Pulp**: soft connective tissue inside the tooth containing nerves, blood vessels, immune cells, and odontoblasts.

[EMPHASIS] **Apical foramen**: the main portal where nerves and vessels enter the tooth at the root tip.

[EMPHASIS] **Apical constriction**: the narrowest part of the canal system near the apex—often considered a key landmark for working length.

[EMPHASIS] **Periodontal ligament (PDL)**: connective tissue between root and bone—important in diagnosing periapical conditions.

Disease & Diagnosis Terms

[EMPHASIS] **Reversible pulpitis**: inflammation where the pulp can recover—classically sharp pain to cold that resolves quickly.

[EMPHASIS] **Irreversible pulpitis**: inflammation beyond recovery—often lingering pain, spontaneous pain, or pain that’s difficult to localize early on.

[EMPHASIS] **Pulp necrosis**: non-vital pulp—often no response to sensibility tests, may be asymptomatic until apical disease develops.

[EMPHASIS] **Apical periodontitis**: inflammation at the apex, usually due to infected/necrotic pulp—may be symptomatic or asymptomatic.

Treatment Terms

[EMPHASIS] **Working length**: the measured distance for cleaning and shaping—critical for effective disinfection and preventing over-instrumentation.

[EMPHASIS] **Chemomechanical debridement**: combined action of instruments and irrigants to remove tissue, microbes, and debris.

[EMPHASIS] **Obturation**: filling and sealing the cleaned canal system, typically with gutta-percha and a sealer.

[EMPHASIS] **Coronal seal**: the quality of the restoration sealing the tooth from the top—vital for preventing reinfection.

7) Common Misconceptions to Correct Early

1. “Root canal treatment removes all bacteria.”

Reality: We reduce bacterial levels dramatically, then **seal** to prevent regrowth and reinfection.

2. “If the radiograph looks normal, the pulp must be fine.”

Reality: Pulpal disease can exist without radiographic changes, especially early.

Quick Summary: Misconceptions

Endodontics is **microbial management + sealing**, not “sterilization.”

Diagnosis is **multi-factor**, not radiograph-only.

Anatomy is **complex**, so technique must be systematic.
