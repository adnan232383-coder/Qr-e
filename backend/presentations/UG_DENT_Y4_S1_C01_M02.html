  • --- ## 2) A Practical Framework: How to Think Through Any Periodontal Case Before the cases, here’s a repeatable framework you can use for virtually every patient: ### Step 1: Identify the Chief Concern + Context Why are they here today? Pain, bleeding, esthetics, mobility, implant maintenance, or “my dentist said I have gum disease”? ### Step 2: Collect Key Diagnostic Data - Full periodontal charting (probing depths, recession, bleeding on probing) - [EMPHASIS]Clinical attachment level[/EMPHASIS] interpretation - Mobility, furcations, mucogingival findings - Plaque control assessment - Radiographs: crestal bone levels, patterns of bone loss, calculus, furcation involvement ### Step 3: Determine Disease Type and Severity Is it gingivitis vs periodontitis? Localized vs generalized? Stable vs progressive? ### Step 4: Risk and Modifying Factors Smoking, diabetes, medications, stress, bruxism, genetics, and local plaque-retentive factors.
  • ### Step 5: Phase-Based Treatment Plan - Phase I: patient education, risk factor control, scaling and root planing, adjuncts when indicated - Re-evaluation: response, residual pockets, compliance - Phase II: surgery if needed (access, regenerative, resective, mucogingival) - Maintenance: [EMPHASIS]Supportive periodontal therapy[/EMPHASIS] [PAUSE] **Key Point Summary:** Periodontal care is not “cleaning plus lecture.” It’s a diagnostic-driven, phased plan tied to risk and re-evaluation.
