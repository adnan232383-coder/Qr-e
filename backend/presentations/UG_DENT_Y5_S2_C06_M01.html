Key Points 1

  • ## Introduction (Module Opening) Welcome to **Aesthetic Dentistry: Introduction & Fundamentals**.
  • In this module, we’ll build the foundation for everything you’ll do in cosmetic and esthetic dental care—whether you’re planning a simple whitening case, closing a diastema with composite, or coordinating a full smile rehabilitation.
  • Aesthetic dentistry is not just “making teeth look whiter.” It’s the careful blending of **biology, function, and appearance**—so the final outcome is attractive, healthy, and stable over time.

  • [PAUSE] --- ## Section 1: What Is Aesthetic Dentistry? (Big Picture Overview) Aesthetic dentistry focuses on **optimizing dental and facial appearance** while respecting **oral health** and [EMPHASIS]occlusion[/EMPHASIS].

Key Points 5

  • It overlaps with restorative dentistry, prosthodontics, periodontics, orthodontics, and even facial aesthetics.
  • The “art” part is important—but it must be grounded in **diagnosis** and **risk management**.
  • A helpful way to think about it is the “triangle” of successful aesthetic care: - **Biology**: enamel preservation, pulp health, periodontal stability - **Function**: occlusal scheme, guidance, parafunction management - **Appearance**: smile harmony, tooth form, color, and soft tissue framing If you ignore any corner of this triangle, the result might look good temporarily—but it won’t last.
